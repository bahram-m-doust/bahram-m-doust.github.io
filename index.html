Research

Research Interests

My research explores AI-driven design pipelines that bridge the gap between conceptual imagery and engineering documentation. I investigate how multimodal AI systems can automate the translation of 2D concept renders into dimensioned technical drawings (ISO 129-1), 3D meshes, material specifications, and fabrication-ready documentation — enabling designers to focus on creative decision-making while AI handles repetitive drafting and asset generation.

Human-AI Interaction in Design

How designers interact with AI-generated technical outputs. Evaluating trust, accuracy perception, and workflow integration of AI design agents.

Computational Design Pipelines

End-to-end systems connecting concept imagery to manufacturing documentation through VLM analysis, generative image models, and 3D reconstruction.

AI-Assisted Manufacturing Documentation

Automated generation of dimensioned blueprints, bill of materials, and fabrication specifications with regional standards compliance (ISO, ESMA).

Spatial Computing & XR

Virtual and extended reality experiences for architectural visualization, brand environments, and interactive spatial design evaluation.

Flagship R&D Project

AIQ Studio — Detail Design Agent

An AI-driven pipeline that ingests a single 2D concept image and outputs dimensioned technical blueprints, exploded isometric views, section cuts, bill of materials with regional manufacturing standards, and render-ready 3D meshes (.glb) — reducing concept-to-execution time from days to minutes.

Current Limitations & Future Work

Dimensional accuracy is approximate (~85-90% for standard furniture) and requires validation. Current implementation relies on external APIs (OpenRouter, Replicate) rather than custom-trained models. Active development: RhinoCommon API integration for parametric dimensioning, accuracy benchmarking against ground-truth measurements, and user evaluation studies with practicing designers.

Publication: In preparation — targeting eCAADe 2026 / ACADIA 2026
